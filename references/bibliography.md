# Working Bibliography

## Market Efficiency in Professional Tennis: Can Machine Learning Models Outperform Betting Odds?

This bibliography collects the sources used or consulted for the project. Entries are grouped by theme and listed alphabetically by first author within each section. Annotations are brief and indicate how a source informs the paper.

---

## Foundational Theory: Market Efficiency and Betting Markets

Fama, E. F. (1970). Efficient capital markets: A review of theory and empirical work. *Journal of Finance*, *25*(2), 383–417. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1970.tb00518.x

> The canonical statement of the efficient market hypothesis. Provides the organising framework for our market-efficiency tests.

Sauer, R. D. (1998). The economics of wagering markets. *Journal of Economic Literature*, *36*(4), 2021–2064. https://doi.org/10.1257/jel.36.4.2021

> Comprehensive survey of efficiency tests, the favourite-longshot bias, and bookmaker industrial organisation up to the late 1990s.

Surowiecki, J. (2004). *The wisdom of crowds: Why the many are smarter than the few and how collective wisdom shapes business, economies, societies, and nations*. Doubleday.

> Popular but influential statement of the conditions under which aggregate beliefs outperform individual forecasts; helpful background for interpreting market odds as information aggregators.

Thaler, R. H., & Ziemba, W. T. (1988). Anomalies: Parimutuel betting markets: Racetracks and lotteries. *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, *2*(2), 161–174. https://doi.org/10.1257/jep.2.2.161

> Introduces betting markets as natural laboratories for testing market efficiency and documents the favourite-longshot bias.

Vaughan Williams, L. (1999). Information efficiency in betting markets: A survey. *Bulletin of Economic Research*, *51*(1), 1–30. https://doi.org/10.1111/1467-8586.00069

> Surveys weak-, semi-strong-, and strong-form efficiency tests in betting markets; a standard reference for the structure of our literature review.

Vaughan Williams, L. (Ed.). (2005). *Information efficiency in financial and betting markets*. Cambridge University Press.

> Edited volume with survey chapters by leading researchers; useful for the historical development of betting-market efficiency research.

Wolfers, J., & Zitzewitz, E. (2004). Prediction markets. *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, *18*(2), 107–126. https://doi.org/10.1257/0895330041371321

> Definitive survey of prediction markets as incentive-compatible belief-aggregation mechanisms.

## Bookmaker Pricing, Margins, and Market Microstructure

Angelini, G., De Angelis, L., & Singleton, C. (2022). Informational efficiency and behaviour within in-play prediction markets. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *38*(1), 282–299. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2021.05.001

> Shows how momentum and scoreline biases create predictable mispricing in tennis in-play markets; useful for discussing live-betting efficiency.

Clarke, S. R., Kovalchik, S. A., & Ingram, M. (2017). Adjusting bookmaker's odds to allow for overround. *American Journal of Sports Science*, *5*(6), 45–49. https://doi.org/10.11648/j.ajss.20170506.12

> Compares methods for removing bookmaker margins and recommends the power method; directly informs our odds-de-vigging procedure.

Fischer, K., & Schmal, W. B. (2025). Pricing in response to new information: The case of betting markets. *Economic Inquiry*, *63*(1), 236–264.

> Natural experiment using COVID-19 ghost games to test how quickly betting markets incorporate new information.

Forrest, D., & McHale, I. G. (2007). Anyone for tennis (betting)? *The European Journal of Finance*, *13*(8), 751–768. https://doi.org/10.1080/13518470701705669

> Early large-scale study of the tennis betting market documenting a longshot bias and establishing tennis as an efficiency-testing ground.

Garcia, D., Lillo, F., & López de Prado, M. (2017). Beating the bookies with their own numbers—And how the online sports betting market is rigged. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1710.02824*. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.1710.02824

> Documents how bookmakers restrict winning accounts, raising important caveats about the real-world scalability of profitable strategies.

Hegarty, T., & Whelan, K. (2024). Comparing two methods for testing the efficiency of sports betting markets. *Sports Economics Review*, *3*, Article 100019. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.serev.2024.100019

> Compares probability-normalisation and inverse-odds tests on large tennis and soccer samples; supports our choice of evaluation methodology.

Hubáček, O., Šourek, G., & Železný, F. (2019). Exploiting sports-betting market using machine learning. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *35*(2), 783–796. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2019.01.001

> Key methodological critique: models that correlate too strongly with bookmaker predictions cannot find value bets. Important for our model-design discussion.

Johansen, K. (2020). *Empirical study of information efficiency in betting markets: Evidence from men's professional tennis* [Master's thesis, Copenhagen Business School]. https://research-api.cbs.dk/ws/portalfiles/portal/66771737/1058968_Information_Efficiency_in_Tennis_Betting_Markets.pdf

> Large-sample analysis of weak-form efficiency and the favourite-longshot bias in men's tennis; useful supplementary evidence.

Koning, R. H., & Zijm, R. (2023). Betting market efficiency and prediction in binary choice models. *Annals of Operations Research*, *325*, 135–148. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10479-022-04722-3

> Proposes regression-based tests for residual favourite-longshot bias in binary-outcome betting markets; applicable to tennis.

Levitt, S. D. (2004). Why are gambling markets organised so differently from financial markets? *The Economic Journal*, *114*(495), 223–246. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1468-0297.2004.00207.x

> Shows that bookmakers actively exploit bettor biases rather than simply balance books; essential for understanding price setting.

Lindroos, J. (2016). *Efficiency of the exchange betting market for men's professional tennis: Is there a favourite-longshot bias?* [Master's thesis, Aalto University School of Business]. https://aaltodoc.aalto.fi/items/4fa370de-ab49-464b-95d8-1379357dbd44

> Exchange-market evidence suggesting the favourite-longshot bias may be partly a bookmaker-margin artefact.

Makropoulou, V., & Markellos, R. N. (2011). What causes the favorite-longshot bias? Further evidence from tennis (MPRA Paper No. 47905). University Library of Munich, Germany. https://mpra.ub.uni-muenchen.de/47905/

> Large tennis sample showing that the favourite-longshot bias varies with match type in ways consistent with information asymmetry.

Newall, P. W. S., & Cortis, D. (2021). Are sports bettors biased toward longshots, favorites, or both? A literature review. *Risks*, *9*(1), Article 22. https://doi.org/10.3390/risks9010022

> Review arguing that the direction of bias depends on the probability range offered by each sport.

Ottaviani, M., & Sørensen, P. N. (2008). The favorite-longshot bias: An overview of the main explanations. In W. A. S. Donald & L. V. Williams (Eds.), *Handbook of sports and lottery markets* (pp. 83–101). Elsevier.

> Definitive survey of competing theoretical explanations for the favourite-longshot bias.

Ottaviani, M., & Sørensen, P. N. (2010). Noise, information, and the favorite-longshot bias in parimutuel predictions. *American Economic Journal: Microeconomics*, *2*(1), 58–85. https://doi.org/10.1257/mic.2.1.58

> Information-theoretic model explaining when and why the favourite-longshot bias can reverse.

Plott, C. R., Wit, J., & Yang, W. C. (2003). Parimutuel betting markets as information aggregation devices: Experimental results. *Economic Theory*, *22*(2), 311–351. https://doi.org/10.1007/s00199-002-0306-7

> Experimental evidence that betting markets aggregate information effectively in simple settings but less so as complexity increases.

Shin, H. S. (1991). Optimal betting odds against insider traders. *The Economic Journal*, *101*(408), 1179–1185. https://doi.org/10.2307/2234887

Shin, H. S. (1992). Prices of state contingent claims with insider traders, and the favourite-longshot bias. *The Economic Journal*, *102*(411), 426–435. https://doi.org/10.2307/2234526

Shin, H. S. (1993). Measuring the incidence of insider trading in a market for state-contingent claims. *The Economic Journal*, *103*(420), 1141–1153. https://doi.org/10.2307/2234240

> Seminal three-paper series deriving the favourite-longshot bias as a supply-side response to insider trading; foundational for bookmaker-pricing models.

Smith, M. A., Paton, D., & Vaughan Williams, L. (2006). Market efficiency in person-to-person betting. *Economica*, *73*(292), 673–689. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1468-0335.2006.00502.x

> Tests efficiency on Betfair and shows how exchange market structure alters favourite-longshot bias patterns.

Snowberg, E., & Wolfers, J. (2010). Explaining the favorite-longshot bias: Is it risk-love or misperceptions? *Journal of Political Economy*, *118*(4), 723–746. https://doi.org/10.1086/655809

> Elegant test based on compound bets favouring probability misperceptions over risk-loving preferences as the demand-side driver of the bias.

Weitzman, M. (1965). Utility analysis and group behaviour: An empirical study. *Journal of Political Economy*, *73*(1), 18–26. https://doi.org/10.1086/259016

> Classic neoclassical explanation of the favourite-longshot bias via locally risk-loving preferences; complements Snowberg & Wolfers (2010).

UK Gambling Commission / Smart Betting Club. (2025). Gambling Commission lifts lid on account restrictions. https://smartbettingclub.com/blog/gambling-commission-restrictions-data/

> Official regulatory data showing that a large share of restricted accounts are profitable; important real-world caveat for betting-strategy results.

Whelan, K., & Hegarty, T. (2023). Calculating the bookmaker's margin: Why bets lose more on average than you are warned (UCD Centre for Economic Research Working Paper WP23/04; MPRA Paper No. 116924). https://mpra.ub.uni-muenchen.de/116924/

> Large-scale analysis showing that conventional overround formulas understate bettors' actual expected losses, especially for longshots.

## Tennis Forecasting: Models, Rankings, and Ratings

Angelini, G., Candila, V., & De Angelis, L. (2022). Weighted Elo rating for tennis match predictions. *European Journal of Operational Research*, *297*(1), 120–132. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ejor.2021.04.011

> Introduces a margin-of-victory-weighted Elo system and reports small positive returns on best-odds betting; useful benchmark for rating systems.

Barnett, T., & Clarke, S. R. (2005). Combining player statistics to predict outcomes of tennis matches. *IMA Journal of Management Mathematics*, *16*(2), 113–120. https://doi.org/10.1093/imaman/dpi001

> Introduces opponent-adjusted serve/return statistics and a Markov-chain match model; foundational for common-opponent approaches.

del Corral, J., & Prieto-Rodríguez, J. (2010). Are differences in ranks good predictors for Grand Slam tennis matches? *International Journal of Forecasting*, *26*(3), 551–563. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2009.12.006

> Older but useful ranking-based baseline limited to Grand Slams; shows the limits of official rankings as standalone predictors.

Easton, S., & Uylangco, K. (2010). Forecasting outcomes in tennis matches using within-match betting markets. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *26*(3), 564–575. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2009.12.009

> Compares the Klaassen–Magnus point model with in-play odds; small sample but high correlation suggests strong market efficiency.

Elo, A. E. (1978). *The rating of chessplayers, past and present*. Arco Publishing.

> Original exposition of the Elo rating system; cited for the theoretical foundation of all Elo-based sports ratings.

Gallagher, S. K., Frisoli, K., & Luby, A. (2021). Opening up the court: Analyzing player performance across tennis Grand Slams. *Journal of Quantitative Analysis in Sports*, *17*(4), 255–271. https://doi.org/10.1515/jqas-2019-0015

> Mixed-effects analysis of measurable surface-specific effects at the individual-player level; provides reproducible code.

Glickman, M. E. (1999). Parameter estimation in large dynamic paired comparison experiments. *Journal of the Royal Statistical Society: Series C (Applied Statistics)*, *48*(3), 377–394. https://doi.org/10.1111/1467-9876.00158

> Introduces the Glicko rating system with rating deviation and Bayesian updating; useful methodological background on alternatives to Elo.

Gorgi, P., Koopman, S. J., & Lit, R. (2019). The analysis and forecasting of tennis matches by using a high dimensional dynamic model. *Journal of the Royal Statistical Society: Series A*, *182*(4), 1393–1409. https://doi.org/10.1111/rssa.12464

> State-space model for time-varying player abilities; represents the current frontier of statistical tennis forecasting.

Herbrich, R., Minka, T., & Graepel, T. (2007). TrueSkill(TM): A Bayesian skill rating system. In B. Schölkopf, J. Platt, & T. Hoffman (Eds.), *Advances in neural information processing systems 19* (pp. 569–576). MIT Press. https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2006/hash/f44ee263952e65b7290d2b200da89501-Abstract.html

> Generalises Elo/Glicko with draw modelling and arbitrary competitor numbers; useful for rating-system background.

Ingram, M. (2019). A point-based Bayesian hierarchical model to predict the outcome of tennis matches. *Journal of Quantitative Analysis in Sports*, *15*(4), 313–325. https://doi.org/10.1515/jqas-2018-0008

> Bayesian hierarchical model with dynamic surface-specific skills; provides uncertainty quantification valuable for betting applications.

Klaassen, F. J. G. M., & Magnus, J. R. (2001). Are points in tennis independent and identically distributed? Evidence from a dynamic binary panel data model. *Journal of the American Statistical Association*, *96*(454), 500–509. https://doi.org/10.1198/016214501753168157

> Tests the i.i.d. assumption with 90,000 Wimbledon points; deviations are small enough that i.i.d. remains a useful approximation.

Klaassen, F. J. G. M., & Magnus, J. R. (2003). Forecasting the winner of a tennis match. *European Journal of Operational Research*, *148*(2), 257–267. https://doi.org/10.1016/S0377-2217(02)00682-3

> Foundational stochastic tennis forecasting model; benchmark for subsequent point-based and match-level predictions.

Knottenbelt, W. J., Spanias, D., & Madurska, A. M. (2012). A common-opponent stochastic model for predicting the outcome of professional tennis matches. *Computers & Mathematics with Applications*, *64*(12), 3820–3827. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.camwa.2012.03.005

> Hierarchical Markov model using common-opponent statistics to adjust for strength of schedule.

Kovalchik, S. A. (2016). Searching for the GOAT of tennis win prediction. *Journal of Quantitative Analysis in Sports*, *12*(3), 127–138. https://doi.org/10.1515/jqas-2015-0059

> Comprehensive benchmark of 11 published models against betting odds; central reference for our literature review.

Kovalchik, S. A. (2020). Extension of the Elo rating system to margin of victory. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *36*(4), 1329–1341. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2020.01.006

> Extends Elo to incorporate margin of victory; bridges match-result ratings and point-level performance data.

Kovalchik, S. A., & Ingram, M. (2018). Estimating the duration of professional tennis matches for varying formats. *Journal of Quantitative Analysis in Sports*, *14*(1), 13–23. https://doi.org/10.1515/jqas-2017-0077

> Monte Carlo simulation showing that longer formats favour the better player and reduce upset probability.

Kovalchik, S. A., & Reid, M. (2019). A calibration method with dynamic updates for within-match forecasting of wins in tennis. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *35*(2), 756–766. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2017.11.008

> Strong methodology for dynamic in-play calibration; informs our discussion of Brier score and log-loss evaluation.

Leitner, C., Zeileis, A., & Hornik, K. (2010). Forecasting sports tournaments by ratings of (prob)abilities: A comparison for the Euro 2008. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *26*(3), 471–481. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2009.10.004

> Soccer-focused but introduces a general paired-comparison framework widely adapted for tennis forecasting.

McHale, I. G., & Morton, A. (2011). A Bradley-Terry type model for forecasting tennis match results. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *27*(2), 619–630. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijforecast.2010.04.004

> Dynamic paired-comparison model with surface effects; useful methodological reference for rating-based forecasts.

Newton, P. K., & Aslam, K. (2009). Monte Carlo tennis: A stochastic Markov chain model. *Journal of Quantitative Analysis in Sports*, *5*(3), Article 1. https://doi.org/10.2202/1559-0410.1183

> Extends the analytical Markov model to Monte Carlo simulation, capturing match-to-match variability and surface effects.

Newton, P. K., & Keller, J. B. (2005). Probability of winning at tennis I. Theory and data. *Studies in Applied Mathematics*, *114*(3), 241–269. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.0022-2526.2005.01547.x

> Derives exact recursive formulas for game, set, and match probabilities under i.i.d. assumptions.

O'Malley, A. J. (2008). Probability formulas and statistical analysis in tennis. *Journal of Quantitative Analysis in Sports*, *4*(2), Article 15. https://doi.org/10.2202/1559-0410.1100

> Standard reference for closed-form tennis probability calculations including tiebreaks and advantage sets.

Vaughan Williams, L., Liu, C., Dixon, L., & Gerrard, H. (2021). How well do Elo-based ratings predict professional tennis matches? *Journal of Quantitative Analysis in Sports*, *17*(2), 91–105. https://doi.org/10.1515/jqas-2019-0110

> Direct comparison of Elo, surface Elo, official rankings, and betting odds across Grand Slams; essential benchmark.

Wilkens, S. (2021). Sports prediction and betting models in the machine learning age: The case of tennis. *Journal of Sports Analytics*, *7*(2), 99–117. https://doi.org/10.3233/JSA-200463

> Extensive ML study finding an accuracy ceiling near 70% and showing that betting odds contain most predictive information.

## Machine Learning: Methodology, Ensembles, and Tabular Data

Bergmeir, C., & Benítez, J. M. (2012). On the use of cross-validation for time series predictor evaluation. *Information Sciences*, *191*, 192–213. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ins.2011.12.028

> Shows why standard k-fold cross-validation fails for time series and validates blocked/hv-block alternatives.

Breiman, L. (1996). Bagging predictors. *Machine Learning*, *24*(2), 123–140. https://doi.org/10.1023/A:1018054314350

> Foundational paper on bootstrap aggregating; background for random forests and variance-reduction ensembles.

Breiman, L. (2001). Random forests. *Machine Learning*, *45*(1), 5–32. https://doi.org/10.1023/A:1010933404324

> Introduces random forests with out-of-bag error and variable importance; standard reference for tree ensembles.

Borisov, V., Leemann, T., Seßler, K., Haug, J., Pawelczyk, M., & Kasneci, G. (2022). Deep neural networks and tabular data: A survey. *IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems*, *35*(2), 1–21. https://doi.org/10.1109/TNNLS.2022.3229161

> Comprehensive survey concluding that gradient-boosted trees still dominate most tabular benchmarks; supports our algorithm choice.

Bunker, R., & Susnjak, T. (2022). The application of machine learning techniques for predicting match results in team sport: A review. *Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research*, *73*, 1285–1322. https://doi.org/10.1613/jair.1.13509

> Major review identifying feature engineering as the single most important determinant of sports-prediction performance.

Chen, T., & Guestrin, C. (2016). XGBoost: A scalable tree boosting system. In *Proceedings of the 22nd ACM SIGKDD international conference on knowledge discovery and data mining* (pp. 785–794). ACM. https://doi.org/10.1145/2939672.2939785

> Highly influential gradient-boosting implementation; standard reference alongside LightGBM.

Friedman, J., Hastie, T., & Tibshirani, R. (2000). Additive logistic regression: A statistical view of boosting. *Annals of Statistics*, *28*(2), 337–407. https://doi.org/10.1214/aos/1016218222

> Reframes boosting as coordinate descent on exponential loss; statistical foundation for gradient boosting.

Friedman, J. H. (2001). Greedy function approximation: A gradient boosting machine. *Annals of Statistics*, *29*(5), 1189–1232. https://doi.org/10.1214/aos/1013203451

> Original derivation of gradient boosting as functional gradient descent; essential theoretical foundation.

Friedman, J. H. (2002). Stochastic gradient boosting. *Computational Statistics & Data Analysis*, *38*(4), 367–378. https://doi.org/10.1016/S0167-9473(01)00065-2

> Introduces subsampling for variance reduction; completes the theoretical triad underlying modern boosting libraries.

Grinsztajn, L., Oyallon, E., & Varoquaux, G. (2022). Why do tree-based models still outperform deep learning on typical tabular data? In S. Koyejo, S. Mohamed, A. Agarwal, D. Belgrave, K. Cho, & A. Oh (Eds.), *Advances in neural information processing systems 35* (pp. 507–520). Curran Associates. https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2022/hash/0378c7692da36807bdec87ab043cdadc-Abstract-Conference.html

> Rigorous analysis identifying why tree ensembles dominate typical tabular datasets; directly relevant to our modelling choice.

Ke, G., Meng, Q., Finley, T., Wang, T., Chen, W., Ma, W., Ye, Q., & Liu, T.-Y. (2017). LightGBM: A highly efficient gradient boosting decision tree. In I. Guyon, U. V. Luxburg, S. Bengio, H. Wallach, R. Fergus, S. Vishwanathan, & R. Garnett (Eds.), *Advances in neural information processing systems 30* (pp. 3146–3154). Curran Associates. https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2017/hash/6449f44a102fde848669bdd9eb6b76fa-Abstract.html

> Introduces GOSS and EFB; likely primary algorithm for our models.

López de Prado, M. (2018). *Advances in financial machine learning*. John Wiley & Sons.

> Standard reference on purged k-fold cross-validation and embargo methods for temporally ordered data.

Racine, J. (2000). Consistent cross-validatory model-selection for dependent data: hv-block cross-validation. *Journal of Econometrics*, *99*(1), 39–61. https://doi.org/10.1016/S0304-4076(00)00030-0

> Foundational econometrics paper on dependent-data cross-validation; precursor to purged cross-validation.

Arora, S., et al. (2025). Hidden leaks in time series forecasting: How data leakage affects LSTM evaluation across configurations and validation strategies. *arXiv preprint arXiv:2512.06932*. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2512.06932

> Recent experimental analysis showing that 10-fold cross-validation is especially vulnerable to leakage in time-series settings; reinforces our use of chronological splits.

van der Laan, M. J., Polley, E. C., & Hubbard, A. E. (2007). Super learner. *Statistical Applications in Genetics and Molecular Biology*, *6*(1), Article 25. https://doi.org/10.2202/1544-6115.1309

> Formalises stacking as an oracle-efficient ensemble; theoretical foundation for our stacked models.

Wolpert, D. H. (1992). Stacked generalization. *Neural Networks*, *5*(2), 241–259. https://doi.org/10.1016/S0893-6080(05)80023-1

> Introduces stacking as a meta-learning paradigm; background for ensemble design.

## Forecast Evaluation, Calibration, and Betting Strategy

Boulier, B. L., & Stekler, H. O. (1999). Are sports seedings good predictors?: An evaluation. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *15*(1), 83–91. https://doi.org/10.1016/S0169-2070(98)00061-8

> Pre-2000 seeding evaluation across multiple sports; useful early baseline.

Brier, G. W. (1950). Verification of forecasts expressed in terms of probability. *Monthly Weather Review*, *78*(1), 1–3. https://doi.org/10.1175/1520-0493(1950)078<0001:VOFEIT>2.0.CO;2

> Original paper introducing the Brier score; standard metric for our probabilistic forecast evaluation.

Candila, V., & Palazzo, L. (2020). Neural networks and betting strategies for tennis. *Risks*, *8*(3), Article 68. https://doi.org/10.3390/risks8030068

> Neural-network betting study with subset-dependent returns; useful as a contrast to more robust ML benchmarks.

Depken, C. A., Harris, M. S., & MacDonald, D. N. (2015). Psychological momentum in tennis: A test of the anti-momentum hypothesis. *Journal of Sports Economics*, *16*(8), 860–874. https://doi.org/10.1177/1527002513496212

> Econometric evidence for psychological momentum in best-of-three matches; informs momentum-feature discussion.

Dumovic, M., & Howarth, T. (2018). *Tennis match predictions using neural networks* (CS230 Project Report, Stanford University). https://cs230.stanford.edu/projects_spring_2018/reports/8290687.pdf

> Student project supporting the ~70% accuracy ceiling; useful as a cautionary note on deep-learning hype.

Galekwa, R. M., Tshimula, J. M., Tajeuna, E. G., & Kyandoghere, K. (2024). A systematic review of machine learning in sports betting: Techniques, challenges, and future directions. *arXiv preprint arXiv:2410.21484*. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2410.21484

> PRISMA-guided review of 219 articles; useful overview but should be verified against primary sources.

Gao, Z., & Kowalczyk, A. (2021). Random forest model identifies serve strength as a key predictor of tennis match outcome. *Journal of Sports Analytics*, *7*(4), 255–262. https://doi.org/10.3233/JSA-200543

> Claims high accuracy but compares it with odds using accuracy rather than proper probabilistic metrics; use with caution.

Guo, C., Pleiss, G., Sun, Y., & Weinberger, K. Q. (2017). On calibration of modern neural networks. In D. Precup & Y. W. Teh (Eds.), *Proceedings of the 34th international conference on machine learning* (pp. 1321–1330). PMLR. https://proceedings.mlr.press/v70/guo17a.html

> Introduces expected calibration error and temperature scaling; informs our calibration assessment.

Kelly, J. L., Jr. (1956). A new interpretation of information rate. *Bell System Technical Journal*, *35*, 917–926. https://doi.org/10.1002/j.1538-7305.1956.tb03809.x

> Original derivation of the Kelly criterion; mathematical foundation for optimal bet sizing.

Naeini, M. P., Cooper, G., & Hauskrecht, M. (2015). Obtaining well calibrated probabilities using Bayesian binning. In *Proceedings of the 29th AAAI conference on artificial intelligence* (pp. 2901–2907). AAAI Press. https://doi.org/10.1609/aaai.v29i1.9602

> Proposes Bayesian binning into quantiles; useful for understanding calibration metrics.

Platt, J. C. (1999). Probabilistic outputs for support vector machines and comparisons to regularized likelihood methods. In A. J. Smola, P. Bartlett, B. Schölkopf, & D. Schuurmans (Eds.), *Advances in large margin classifiers* (pp. 61–74). MIT Press.

> Introduces sigmoid scaling for classifier calibration; widely used post-hoc calibration technique.

Ramirez, P. (2021). Betting on a buzz, mispricing and inefficiency in online sportsbooks (Economics Discussion Paper EMDP 2021-10). University of Reading. https://www.reading.ac.uk/web/files/economics/emdp202110.pdf

> Creative alternative-data approach using Wikipedia buzz; narrow profitable window and working-paper status limit generalisability.

Thorp, E. O. (2008). The Kelly criterion in blackjack, sports betting, and the stock market. In S. A. Zenios & W. T. Ziemba (Eds.), *Handbook of asset and liability management* (Vol. 1, pp. 385–428). North-Holland.

> Practical formulas for Kelly betting, fractional Kelly, and risk-of-ruin trade-offs.

Walsh, J., & Joshi, A. (2024). Model calibration in sports betting. *arXiv preprint arXiv:2410.21484v1*. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2410.21484

> Argues that calibration matters more than raw accuracy for betting returns; working paper cited in Galekwa et al. (2024).

Wheatcroft, E. (2019). Evaluating probabilistic forecasts of football matches: The case against the ranked probability score (LSE CATS Working Paper; arXiv:1908.08980). https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.1908.08980

> Critique of the ranked probability score; insights transferable to tennis probabilistic evaluation.

Zadrozny, B., & Elkan, C. (2002). Transforming classifier scores into accurate multiclass probability estimates. In *Proceedings of the eighth ACM SIGKDD international conference on Knowledge discovery and data mining* (pp. 694–699). ACM. https://doi.org/10.1145/775047.775151
