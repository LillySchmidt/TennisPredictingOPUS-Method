# Notes on Discarded Sources

## Market Efficiency in Professional Tennis: Can Machine Learning Models Outperform Betting Odds?

The following sources were reviewed but excluded from the working bibliography. Reasons are kept brief and indicate why each was judged insufficient for this paper.

---

## A

Arik, S. O., & Pfister, T. (2019). TabNet: Attentive interpretable tabular learning. *arXiv:1908.07442*.

> Too niche. The broader "trees versus deep learning for tabular data" question is already covered by Borisov et al. (2022) and Grinsztajn et al. (2022).

---

## B

Betting-Forum.com. (2026). The overfitting problem: Why backtested betting systems fail in production. https://www.betting-forum.com/threads/the-overfitting-problem-why-backtested-betting-systems-fail-in-production.47444/

> Unverified community forum post with no disclosed methodology or author credentials.

Bojer, C. S., & Meldgaard, J. P. (2021). Kaggle Grandmaster series: How to approach Kaggle competitions. *Kaggle Blog*.

> Industry blog post rather than a peer-reviewed source; stacking theory is better supported by van der Laan et al. (2007).

Buchdahl, J. / Football-Data.co.uk. (n.d.). Datasets and analysis on opening vs. closing odds.

> Football-centric data infrastructure; redundant given the tennis-specific data sources already retained.

---

## C

Cain, M., Law, D., & Peel, D. (2000). The favourite-longshot bias and market efficiency in UK football betting. *Scottish Journal of Political Economy*, *47*(1), 25–36.

> Football-focused and redundant with tennis-specific sources on the same bias.

Council of Europe. (2019). Convention on the manipulation of sports competitions (Macolin Convention).

> Regulatory treaty on match-fixing; outside the core scope of market-efficiency and forecasting methodology.

---

## D

Deutscher, C., et al. (2015). Gender differences in reaction to psychological pressure (IZA Discussion Paper No. 9315).

> Interesting on pressure dynamics but does not address betting markets, model comparison, or market efficiency directly.

---

## E

European Commission. (2012). Study on sports fraud: Match-fixing in sport.

> Policy document on match-fixing; tangential to the core argument and adequately covered by integrity references if needed.

---

## F

Fernandez-Fernandez, J., Sanz-Rivas, D., & Mendez-Villanueva, A. (2009). A review of the activity profile and physiological demands of tennis match play. *Strength and Conditioning Journal*, *31*(4), 39–46.

> Physiology review too far removed from betting-market efficiency; fatigue effects are covered from a modelling perspective by Kovalchik & Ingram (2018).

Fingleton, J., & Waldron, P. (1999). Optimal determination of bookmakers' betting odds: Theory and tests.

> Older working paper; bookmaker risk aversion and pricing are now better covered by Shin (1991–1993), Koning & Zijm (2023), and Whelan & Hegarty (2023).

Forrest, D., & Simmons, R. (2000). Forecasting sport: The behaviour and performance of football tipsters. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *16*(3), 317–331.

> Football tipster study with no direct relevance to tennis; the broader point is covered by efficiency surveys already retained.

Fox Sports. (2026). What is vig in sports betting and how does it work. https://www.foxsports.com/stories/betting/what-is-the-vig

> Basic explainer with no original research; margin concepts are better cited from peer-reviewed literature.

Freitas, V. S. (2024). Optimizing sports outcome prediction through feature engineering and machine learning [Master's thesis, Missouri State University].

> Graduate thesis with lower credibility; feature-engineering principles are covered by Bunker & Susnjak (2022).

Friligkos, A., et al. (2023). Tennis Crystal Ball: ATP match prediction platform.

> Unpublished platform paper with insufficient methodological detail and an unverified accuracy claim.

---

## G

Gollub, J. (2017). Producing win probabilities for professional tennis matches from any score [Bachelor's thesis, Harvard University].

> Undergraduate thesis with an interesting real-time prototype but no peer-reviewed validation; in-play forecasting is covered by Kovalchik & Reid (2019).

Gorishniy, Y., Rubachev, I., Khrulkov, V., & Babenko, A. (2021). Revisiting deep learning models for tabular data. *NeurIPS 2021*.

> Specific architecture paper; the general tabular-versus-deep-learning debate is covered by the Borisov et al. (2022) survey and Grinsztajn et al. (2022).

Groll, A., Ley, C., Schauberger, G., & Van Eetvelde, H. (2019). A hybrid random forest approach for modeling and prediction of international football matches. *International Journal of Forecasting*, *35*(2), 688–705.

> Football-focused; transferable in principle but not needed alongside tennis-specific and general methodology sources.

---

## J

Jullien, B., & Salanié, B. (1994). Measuring the incidence of insider trading: A comment on Shin.

> Narrow technical simplification of Shin's procedure; Shin's method is already covered by the original papers and Clarke et al. (2017).

---

## K

Koessler, F., Noussair, C., & Ziegelmeyer, A. (2012). Information aggregation and belief elicitation in experimental parimutuel betting markets. *Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization*, *83*(2), 195–208.

> Narrow experimental focus on belief elicitation; not directly relevant to fixed-odds tennis forecasting.

Kovacs, M. S., & Baker, L. B. (2014). Recovery interventions and strategies for improved tennis performance. *British Journal of Sports Medicine*, *48*(Suppl 1), i18–i27.

> Sports-medicine review on recovery; too physiologically focused for the core market-efficiency argument.

Kovalchik, S. A. (2015). Grand Slams are short-changing women's tennis. *Significance*, *12*(5), 10–13.

> The same format-effects argument is covered more comprehensively by Kovalchik & Ingram (2018).

---

## P

Pinnacle Sports. (n.d.). Sharp bookmaker business model. [Industry communications].

> Useful contrast in principle but not an independent academic source; the sharp-versus-recreational bookmaker point is covered by Garcia et al. (2017) and Whelan & Hegarty (2023).

Pinnacle Sports Research. (n.d.). Closing line value literature. [Industry blog posts].

> Industry blog posts; the closing-line-value concept is covered by Fischer & Schmal (2025) and Smith et al. (2006).

Popov, S., Morozov, S., & Babenko, A. (2020). Neural oblivious decision ensembles for deep learning on tabular data. *ICLR 2020*.

> Another specific architecture paper; not needed given the broader Borisov et al. (2022) survey.

Punter2Pro. (2026). Avoid betting account restrictions, limits, and closures. https://punter2pro.com/prevent-betting-accounts-restricted-closed/

> Affiliate blog with anecdotal content and promotional tone; account restrictions are better supported by official UK Gambling Commission data.

---

## S

Sackmann, J. (2024). ATP tennis rankings, results, and stats [GitHub repository]. https://github.com/JeffSackmann/tennis_atp

> Redundant with the retained Sackmann (n.d.) GitHub repository entry covering the same data ecosystem.

Sim, M. K., Kim, Y., & Kim, S. (2020). The winning probability of a game and the importance of points in tennis matches. *Research Quarterly for Exercise and Sport*, *91*(3), 523–531.

> Largely redundant with Klaassen & Magnus (2001, 2003); adds limited new insight.

Sleeuwaert, M., & Thiebe, R. (2020). Evaluation of the current state of football result forecasting.

> Master's thesis on football forecasting; lower credibility and the scoring-rules debate is better addressed by Wheatcroft (2019).

Spanias, D., & Knottenbelt, W. J. (2015). Professional tennis: Quantitative models and ranking algorithms [Doctoral thesis, Imperial College London].

> Superseded by peer-reviewed publications from the same authors and more recent tennis forecasting reviews.

Sports Betting Intelligence Unit, UK Gambling Commission. (n.d.). Terms of reference and operational reports.

> UK-specific regulatory documentation; too narrow and regulatory for the core scope.

Stanford CS229. (2017). Real time tennis match prediction using machine learning [Course project].

> Course project with accessible methodology but insufficient rigour for citation; in-play prediction is covered by peer-reviewed sources.

---

## T

Tennis Abstract. (2024). Match Charting Project [Database]. https://www.tennisabstract.com/

> Redundant with the retained Sackmann GitHub repository entry; same data ecosystem.

Tennis Majors. (2025). Surface, climate and tennis betting. https://www.tennismajors.com/others-news/surface-climate-and-tennis-betting-why-conditions-move-the-odds-more-than-you-think-840272.html

> Industry analysis without disclosed data or rigorous methodology; surface effects are covered by Angelini et al. (2022) and Vaughan Williams et al. (2021).

TheOver.ai. (2026). What is overfitting in betting models? https://blog.theover.ai/what-is-overfitting-in-betting-models/

> Generic overfitting advice with no original research; methodological critiques are better cited from Hubáček et al. (2019) and Dumovic & Howarth (2018).

---

## U

UK Gambling Commission / Industry reports on account restrictions. (2016–2024).

> Redundant with the retained UK Gambling Commission / Smart Betting Club (2025) entry, which uses more recent data.

---

## W

Wang, Z., Zhang, Y., & Fu, H. (2014). Autoregressive prediction with rolling mechanism for time series forecasting with small sample size. *Mathematical Problems in Engineering*, Article 572173.

> Narrow focus on rolling-window autoregression; the underlying principle is covered by López de Prado (2018) and Bergmeir & Benítez (2012).

Williams, J., Liu, Y., & Dixon, M. (n.d.). Surface-specific Elo ratings for tennis.

> Citation could not be independently verified; surface-specific Elo is already covered by Vaughan Williams et al. (2021).

---

## Y

Yang, D., Zhang, Q., & Luan, B. (2024). Quantitative study of tennis match momentum and flow based on machine learning. *Transactions on Computer Science and Intelligent Systems Research*.

> Dubious venue and weak claims; psychological momentum is better covered by Depken et al. (2015).

---

## Z

Zhang, Y., et al. (2025). Predicting tennis match outcomes mid-game using machine learning on psychological and physical data. *Journal of Big Data*, *12*, Article 1216.

> Claims of 97.5% mid-game accuracy are inconsistent with the established ~70% ceiling and strongly suggest leakage or overfitting.
